In materials with a high refractive index, known as Cherenkov materials, the speed of light is slower than the speed of electrons. This imbalance allows electrons to emit electromagnetic radiation, creating a blue glow known as Cherenkov radiation. This phenomenon is used in medical imaging and nuclear reactors.
