During winter hibernation, some species of frogs allow up to 65% of their body fluids to freeze. However, the presence of unique ice-binding proteins prevents damage and ensures survival when they thaw again months later. This adaptation helps them survive extreme temperatures while remaining undisturbed for an extended period.
