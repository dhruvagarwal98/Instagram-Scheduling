On October 18, 2019, Koch and Meir made history by completing the first spacewalk conducted entirely by women. The two astronauts spent over seven hours outside the International Space Station, replacing a faulty power controller. This accomplishment served as a great achievement not only for NASA but to women in general, emphasizing inclusion and diversity.
