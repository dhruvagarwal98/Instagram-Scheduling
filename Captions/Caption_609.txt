The cells in the stomach lining have a short lifespan due to rapid acid exposure - so they're rapidly replaced by new ones. This constant cycle keeps the stomach walls at an optimum level of thickness and protects against ulcers or other damaging conditions. It's also why some antacid medications can interfere with this process and cause long term harm.
