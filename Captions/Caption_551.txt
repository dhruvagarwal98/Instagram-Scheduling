While fingerprints have been used for identification purposes for over a century, a recent study has shown that tongue prints can also be used for individual identification. The study found that the ridges and grooves on the tongue are as unique as fingerprints and can be used to identify individuals with an accuracy of 100%. This discovery could have significant implications in the field of forensics as it provides another means of identifying suspects.
