A study at Yale University found that participants rated music higher when they believed it was made by a human musician, regardless of whether it was actually composed by a person or a computer algorithm. This highlights the psychological phenomenon of attribution bias, where people's perceptions and judgments are influenced by their beliefs about the source of information. This could have implications for the music industry and the use of AI in music creation.
