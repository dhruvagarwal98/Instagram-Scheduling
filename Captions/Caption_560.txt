In Indonesia, palm civets eat ripe coffee cherries and then excrete the partially digested beans. These beans are collected, cleaned, roasted and brewed to make Kopi Luwak which sells for anywhere between $100 to $600 per pound. Although considered a delicacy, animal welfare concerns have been raised due to the cruel conditions faced by caged civets in some production facilities.
