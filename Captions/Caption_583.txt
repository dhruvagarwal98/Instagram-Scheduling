During the Renaissance period, high heels became fashionable among men in Europe. However, these shoes were not worn for aesthetic purposes but as a symbol of status and wealth. At that time, only the wealthy could afford expensive shoes with high heels. The higher the heel, the more expensive they were considered. Interestingly, women only started wearing high heels in the 17th century.
