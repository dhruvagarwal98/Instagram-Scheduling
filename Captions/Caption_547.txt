The first computer mouse, invented by Douglas Engelbart in 1964, was constructed entirely out of wood, with a single button and a rolling ball attached to the bottom. The mouse was created as part of Engelbart's vision for a more efficient and user-friendly way of interacting with computers. While the design has certainly evolved since then, the basic concept has remained the same.
