In 2016, Aleix Segura Vendrell of Spain set the world record for the longest time to hold his breath underwater without oxygen assistance. He accomplished this feat by following a strict pre-dive routine including hyperventilating and doing a variety of relaxation techniques. Those between the ages of 18-24 are likely to be interested in extreme sports and adventures, and this record is an impressive display of human endurance.
